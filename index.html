Chapter 2: The Marketer Learns to See

1983 mein, main bahut young aur inexperienced brand manager tha Spinnaker mein, ek startup software company jise maine business school ke baad join kiya tha. Achanak se mere budget mein millions of dollars aagaye, ad reps ke sath fancy lunches (jo maine maange bhi nahi the), aur ek urgent zaroorat: meri amazing team ne jo software banaya tha, uski baat logo tak pahunchana.

Maine wo saara ad ka paisa barbaad kar diya. Ads ne kaam nahi kiya kyunki logo ne ads ko ignore kar diya tha. Par kisi na kisi tarah, software bik gaya.

Itne saalo mein, maine dozens projects launch kiye hain aur businesses aur individuals dono ko goods aur services bechi hain. Maine Jay Levinson (Guerrilla Marketing ke father), Lester Wunderman (direct mail ke godfather), aur Bernadette Jiwa (storytelling ki queen) ke sath kaam kiya hai. Mere ideas ne billion-dollar companies khadi ki hain aur important charities ke liye lagbhag utna hi paisa raise kiya hai.

Zyadatar, meri is journey mein yahi focus raha hai ki ye notice karun ki kya cheez kaam karti hai aur kya nahi karti. Ye trial aur error (zyadatar error) ka ek continuous experiment raha hai, un projects aur organizations ke sath jinki mujhe parwah hai.

...ideas ek insaan se dusre insaan tak failte hain, aur jab aap koi badlaav (change) laate ho, toh aap ek poori community (tribe) ko apne sath jod lete ho.

This Is Marketing: Ek Executive Summary

Jo ideas failte hain, wahi jeette hain.

Marketers badlaav laate hain: us 'smallest viable market' (sabse choti par zaroori audience) ke liye. Wo aise personal aur relevant messages deliver karte hain jiska log wait kar rahe hote hain aur jo wo sach mein sunna chahte hain.

Asli marketers consumers ka use apni company ki problem solve karne ke liye nahi karte; balki wo marketing ka use logo ki problems solve karne ke liye karte hain. Unke andar itni empathy (samajh) hoti hai ki wo jante hain ki unki audience wo nahi chahti jo marketer chahta hai. Audience un cheezon mein believe nahi karti ya unki parwah nahi karti jo marketer ke liye important hain. Aur shayad wo kabhi karenge bhi nahi.

Hamare culture ke core mein 'status' ko lekar hamara belief hota hai—kisi bhi interaction mein hum apna role kya samajhte hain, aur hum aage kahan jaa rahe hain.

Hum apne status roles aur ye decisions (ki hum kisse jude hain aur kispar hamara control hai) use karte hain ye tay karne ke liye ki hume kahan jana hai aur wahan kaise pahunchna hai.

Lagatar, consistent aur baar-baar sunai jane wali kahaniyan, jab ek aisi audience tak pahunchti hain jo aapse align karti hai, toh wo apko unka attention, trust aur action dilaati hain.

Direct marketing aur brand marketing dono ek nahi hain, par dono isi decision par base karti hain ki hume sahi logo ke liye sahi cheez banani hai.

"Hamare jaise log aisi hi cheezein karte hain" — isi line se hum sab culture ko samajhte hain, aur marketers har roz isi idea ke sath deal karte hain.

Ideas ek slope (dhalan) ke zariye aage badhte hain. Wo early adopters ke beech se hote hue nikalte hain...

...early adopters se hote hue ek chasm (badi khaai) ko paar karte hain, aur phir mehnat karke aam janta (masses) tak pahunchte hain. Kabhi-kabhi.

Aajkal attention ek bahut precious resource hai kyunki hamare dimaag mein pehle se hi bahut shor (noise) bhara hai. Smart marketers un logo ke liye chizein aasan bana dete hain jinhe wo target kar rahe hain. Wo apne product (offering) ko aise position karte hain jo logo ke dil ko chhu jaye (resonates) aur yaadgar (memorable) ban jaye.

Sabse badi baat, marketing is cheez se shuru (aur aksar khatam) hoti hai ki hum kya karte hain aur kaise karte hain. Marketing un sab cheezon ke baare mein nahi hai jo product banne aur ship hone ke baad aati hain.

Tumhari tactics (chhoti tricks) thoda bahut fark la sakti hain, par tumhari strategy—tumhara ek tareeke se jeene ka commitment, ek kahani jo sunani hai, aur ek promise jo nibhana hai—ye sab kuch badal sakti hai.

Agar tum badlaav (change) laana chahte ho, toh ek culture banane se shuruat karo. Ek tightly knit group (majbooti se jude hue logo ka group) banane se shuruat karo. Logo ko ek sync (ek soch) mein laane se shuruat karo.

Culture hamesha strategy ko hara deta hai—itna zyada ki culture hi asli strategy hai.

Wo baatein jo marketers jaante hain:

  1. Committed aur creative log duniya badal sakte hain (sach toh ye hai ki sirf wahi badalte hain). Tum isse abhi issi waqt kar sakte ho, aur tum apni soch se bhi kahin zyada bada badlaav la sakte ho.
  2. Tum har kisi ko nahi badal sakte; isliye ye puchna ki, "Ye kiske liye hai?" (Who's it for?) tumhare actions ko focus de sakta hai aur un logo se deal karne mein madad kar sakta hai jo tum par vishwas nahi karte (chahe wo tumhare dimaag ke andar ke nonbelievers hon ya bahar ki duniya ke).
  3. Badlaav hamesha intent (ek saaf maqsad) ke sath sabse aacha hota hai. "Iska fayda kya hai?" (What's it for?) — yahi wo mindset hai jo us kaam ke liye zaroori hai jo sach mein matter karta hai.

Chapter 3

Marketing Logo Ko Badalti Hai Stories, Connections, Aur Experience Ke Zariye

Case Study: VisionSpring — Zarooratmand logo ko chashme (glasses) bechna

Har insaan ke dimaag mein ek kahani (story) chalti rehti hai, ek aisi kahani jiske zariye wo is duniya ko dekhta aur samajhta hai. Sabse hairan karne wali baat ye hai ki har insaan ki wo kahani bilkul alag hoti hai.

Kuch saal pehle, main ek choti si team ke sath India ke ek gaon (village) mein gaya tha, ye samajhne ke liye ki VisionSpring ko apne kaam mein kin challenges ka saamna karna padta hai.

VisionSpring ek social enterprise hai jo duniya bhar ke un ek billion (100 crore) logo tak reading glasses (padhne wale chashme) pahunchane ka kaam karti hai jinhe unki sach mein zaroorat hai, par unke paas wo nahi hain.

Jab aam insaan ki average umar sirf 30 ya 40 saal hua karti thi, tab is baat se koi farq nahi padta tha ki zyadatar logo ko 50 ki umar mein reading glasses ki zaroorat padegi. Par jaise-jaise logo ki average umar (lifespan) badhi hai, bahut se log baki sab tarah se physically healthy aur active hone ke bawajood kaam nahi kar paate—kyunki wo clearly padh nahi sakte ya paas ka kaam (close-up work) nahi kar sakte. Agar aap ek kapde bunne wale (weaver), sunar (jeweler), ya nurse hain, toh bina chashme ke kaam karna lagbhag impossible ho sakta hai.

VisionSpring ki strategy ye hai ki wo bulk mein attractive chashme (glasses) banate hain, wo bhi bahut kam cost par—shayad $2 (lagbhag ₹160) per pair. Aur phir, local ghoomne wale salespeople ke sath milkar, wo in chashmo ko duniya bhar ke gaon (villages) mein le jaate hain, kahan wo inhe lagbhag $3 (₹250) mein bechte hain.

Manufacturing cost aur selling price ke beech ka ye ek dollar ka difference itna kafi hota hai ki usse shipping ka kharcha nikal jaye, local talent ko paise mil jayein, aur organization lagatar grow karti rahe.

Jab humne gaon mein apna table lagaya, toh bahut se log ye dekhne aaye ki aakhir chal kya raha hai. Wo ek bahut garm din ki dopahar thi aur logo ke paas karne ke liye aur kuch khaas nahi tha.

Aadmiyon ne traditional Indian work shirts pehni hui thi, jinpar embroidery thi aur sabke aage ek pocket thi. Main us patle kapde (sheer fabric) ke aar-paar dekh sakta tha ki lagbhag har kisi ki pocket mein Rupaye (rupees) the.

Toh ab mujhe teen baatein pata thi:

  1. Unki umar (age) dekh kar lag raha tha ki inmein se bahut se logo ko chashme ki zaroorat thi. Ye simple biology hai.
  2. Unmein se bahut se logo ne chashma nahi pehna tha aur na hi wo apne sath carry kar rahe the, toh iska matlab unke paas shayad chashma tha hi nahi.
  3. Aur wahan ghoom rahe zyadatar logo ki jeb mein kuch paise the. Halanki, kisi aise insaan ke liye jo din ka sirf $3 kamata hai, ye chashme shayad mehnge lag sakte the, par har ek ke paas cash zaroor available tha.

Ek-ek karke, jaise-jaise gaon wale hamare table par aaye, humne un sabko ek laminated sheet di jispar ek eye test bana tha. Wo test...

...kuch is tarah design kiya gaya tha ki ye un logo ke liye bhi kaam kare jinhe padhna nahi aata tha, chahe wo koi bhi bhasha bolte hon.

Phir, us laminated sheet wale gaon wale (villager) ko ek sample chashma pehnne ko diya gaya aur usne dobara test diya. Wahin par, turant, wo ekdum saaf dekh sakte the. Chashme aise hi toh kaam karte hain. Ye in aadmiyon aur auraton ke liye koi nayi technology nahi thi, aur na hi koi aisi cheez jis par unhe bharosa na ho.

Uske baad, wo sample chashme utaar kar side mein rakh diye gaye, aur customer ko ek sheesha (mirror) diya gaya aur 10 alag-alag styles chunne (choose karne) ka option diya gaya. Har ek chashma bilkul naya tha, choti plastic ki panni mein pack.

Jo log us table par aaye the aur jinhe chashme ki zaroorat thi, unmein se lagbhag ek-tihaai (one-third) logo ne sach mein chashma kharida. Sirf ek-tihaai.

Is baat ne mujhe hairan kar diya. Main dang tha ki 65 percent log jinhe chashme ki zaroorat thi, jinhe pata tha ki unhe zaroorat hai, aur jinke paas kharidne ke liye paise bhi the, wo bas bina kharide wahan se chale gaye.

Khud ko unki jagah rakh kar dekhun, toh main aisi choice karne ka soch bhi nahi sakta tha. Chashmo ki supply ek ghante mein khatam hone wali thi. Price ekdum lajawab tha. Ye trusted technology kaam kar rahi thi. Phir hum kya galat kar rahe the?

Main ek ghante tak dhoop mein baitha raha, is problem ke baare mein gehrayi se sochta raha. Mujhe laga jaise ek marketer ke roop mein mera saara experience aur kaam mujhe is ek moment tak le aaya tha.

Toh maine process mein sirf ek cheez badal di. Ek aisi cheez jisne chashme bikne ki percentage double kar di.

Maine ye kiya: Maine table se saare chashme hata diye.

Line mein khade baaki logo ke liye, jab unhone sample chashma pehna, toh humne kaha, "Ye lijiye aapka naya chashma. Agar ye kaam karta hai aur...

...aapko pasand aate hain, toh please hume teen dollar de dijiye. Agar aapko ye nahi chahiye, toh please inhe wapas kar dijiye."

Bas itna hi.

Humne unki kahani ko is soch se badal diya: "Ye rahi shopping karne ki, aacha dikhne ki, apni nazar wapas paane ki, aur shuru se aakhir tak is process ko enjoy karne ki opportunity" se lekar is soch tak: "Kya aap chahte hain ki hum aapse wo chheen lein jo is waqt aapke paas hai, ya aap un chashmo ko rakhne ke liye paise dena chahenge jo pehle se hi aapke liye aache se kaam kar rahe hain?"

Kuch paane ki chahat (Desire for gain) versus Kuch khone se bachna (Avoidance of loss).

Agar aapne bohot extreme garibi dekhi hai, toh ye imagine karna mushkil hai ki ameer logo ko shopping mein kitna maza aata hai. Us thrill ko feel karna jo aisi cheez kharidne se milta hai jo pehle kabhi nahi kharidi.

Shopping par jana ek risk lene jaisa hai. Hum ek nayi cheez dhoondhne mein apna time aur paisa risk karte hain, ek aisi cheez jo shayad bohot aachi nikal sakti hai. Aur hum ye risk isliye le paate hain kyunki agar hamari choice galat bhi nikli toh koi jaan nahi chali jayegi. Galat hone par hamara raat ka khana (dinner) ya medical checkup nahi chhut jayega.

Aur agar hum galat nikalte bhi hain, toh na sirf hum agle din zinda rahenge, balki kal wapas se shopping karne nikal jayenge.

Doosri taraf, jab mujhe ye realize hua ki shayad baaki log shopping ke baare mein us tarah nahi sochte jaisa main sochta tha, ya jaisa Western opticians sochte the, toh maine cheezon ko alag nazariye se dekha. Shayad jin logo ki hum madad karna chahte the, unke liye kisi nayi cheez ki shopping karna ek maze ki activity nahi, balki ek khatra (threat) tha.

Kisi aam mall mein ghoomne wale zyadatar teenagers is baat par chidh jayenge agar unhe saare chashme try karne na mile, ya unhe apni choice select karne ka option na diya jaye.

Hum mein se zyadatar log kisi aur ka use kiya hua (used) chashma nahi chahenge; hum chahenge ki...

...ekdum fancy aur naye wale (chashme). Bhale hi 'used' (use kiye hue) ka matlab sirf itna ho ki kisi ne usko bas ek baar pehan ke dekha ho. Par ye sochna bilkul helpful nahi hai ki har koi wahi janta hai jo tum jante ho, wahi chahta hai jo tum chahte ho, aur unhi baaton mein yakeen karta hai jinme tum karte ho.

Chashma kaise kharidna chahiye, is baare mein meri kahani (narrative) line mein khade agle gaon wale ki kahani se na toh aachi thi aur na hi buri. Meri kahani bas meri kahani hai, aur agar wo kaam nahi kar rahi, toh uspe zidd pakad ke baithna sirf ghamand (arrogance) hai.

Hum cheezon ko aacha tabhi bana sakte hain jab hum un logo ki sach mein itni parwah karein jinki hum madad kar rahe hain, ki hum us kahani ko imagine kar sakein jo unhe sunne ki zaroorat hai. Humein itna generous (dil daar) hona padega ki hum us kahani ko unke sath share karein, taaki wo log ek aisa action le sakein jispe unhe khud garv (proud) feel ho.

SUV Ke Baare Mein Socho (Consider the SUV)

Is kitab ko padhne wale zyadatar log cars ki marketing nahi karte. Par hum mein se zyadatar logo ne ek na ek car kharidi zaroor hogi.

Sochne wala sawal ye hai: Tumne jo car kharidi, wo wahi wali kyun kharidi?

Wo log jo kabhi zindagi mein off-road driving nahi karenge, wo 90,000 dollar ki Toyota Land Cruiser kyun kharidte hain?

Tesla mein 'Ludicrous Mode' ke liye extra paise kyun dena, jab tumhe pata hai (ya zaroorat hai) ki tum kabhi 0 se 60 mph ki speed 3 second se kam mein nahi pakadne wale?

Apni car mein 3,000 dollar ka mehanga music system (stereo) kyun lagana, jab tum ghar pe sirf 30 dollar ke saste radio pe gaane sunte ho?

Ek aur hairan karne wali baat: Cars ka sabse popular color is baat pe depend karta hai ki kis type ki car kharidi ja rahi hai.

Agar hum sirf 'kaam aane wali cheez' (utility) ko apni kharidari ka main reason banane ke liye taiyar nahi hain...

...jab hum ek 50,000 dollar ki gaadi kharidte waqt decisions lete hain, toh ek perfume ki bottle ya chewing gum ka kya hi chance hai?

Marketing kam paise mein zyada features add karne ki race nahi hai. Marketing hamari ek koshish (quest) hai un logo ki taraf se badlaav (change) laane ki jinki hum madad karte hain, aur hum ye un irrational (bina logic wali) forces ko samajh kar karte hain jo hum sab ko chalati hain.

Wo Quarter-Inch Drill Bit Wala Kissa

Harvard ke marketing professor Theodore Levitt ne ek bahut famous baat kahi thi, "Log quarter-inch ka drill bit (chhed karne wali machine ka aage ka hissa) nahi kharidna chahte. Wo ek quarter-inch ka chhed (hole) chahte hain."

Isse ye lesson milta hai ki drill bit sirf ek feature hai, ek zariya hai apne goal tak pahunchne ka, par log sach mein wahi chhed (hole) chahte hain jo wo banata hai.

Par baat yahan tak hi khatam nahi hoti. Kisi ko chhed (hole) nahi chahiye.

Logo ko asal mein wo shelf chahiye jo us chhed ko karne ke baad deewar par lagegi.

Sach kahun toh, wo asal mein wo feeling chahte hain jab wo dekhenge ki sab kuch kitna saaf aur sameta hua (uncluttered) lag raha hai, jab wo apna saaman us shelf par rakhenge jo deewar par lagi hai, kyunki wahan ab ek quarter-inch ka chhed hai.

Par ruko...

Wo us satisfaction ko bhi chahte hain ye jaan kar ki unhone ye kaam khud kiya hai.

Ya shayad apne status mein wo badhotari (increase) jo unhe tab milegi jab unka partner (spouse) unke kaam ki tareef karega.

Ya fir wo dimaag ki shanti (peace of mind) jo ye jaan kar milti hai ki bedroom ab bikhra hua nahi hai, aur ab wo safe aur clean feel hota hai.

"Log quarter-inch ka drill bit nahi kharidna chahte. Wo safe aur respected feel karna chahte hain."

Ekdum sahi (Bingo).

Log Wo Nahi Chahte Jo Tum Banate Ho

Wo ye chahte hain ki tumhari banayi hui cheez unke liye kya karegi. Wo us feeling ko chahte hain jo wo cheez unhe degi. Aur sach kahun toh, choose karne ke liye aisi bahut zyada feelings nahi hain.

Asal mein, zyadatar marketers wahi same feelings deliver karte hain. Hum bas isko alag-alag tareekon se karte hain, alag services, products, aur stories ke sath. Aur hum ye alag-alag logo ke liye, alag-alag moments par karte hain.

Agar tum kisi ko belonging (apnapan), connection, dimaag ki shanti (peace of mind), status, ya aisi hi koi aur sabse zyada chahi jane wali emotion de sakte ho, toh tumne sach mein kuch aacha kaam kiya hai. Jo cheez tum bechte ho, wo bas un emotions tak pahunchne ka ek raasta (road) hai, aur hum sabko niraash (let down) kar dete hain jab hum sirf choti tricks (tactics) par focus karte hain, results (outcomes) par nahi. "Ye kiske liye hai?" (Who's it for?) aur "Iska fayda kya hai?" (What's it for?) — ye wo do sawal hain jo hamare saare decisions ko guide karte hain.

Stories, Connections, Aur Experiences

Aachi khabar ye hai ki humein sabse naye, chamakte hue digital media shortcuts par depend hone ki zaroorat nahi hai—hamare paas usse bhi zyada powerful, deep, aur hamesha kaam aane wale (timeless) tools maujood hain.

Hum kahaniyan sunate hain. Aisi kahaniyan jo dil ko chhu jayein (resonate) aur waqt ke sath tiki rahein. Aisi kahaniyan jo sach hain, kyunki humne apne actions, apne products aur apni services se unhe sach banaya hai.

Hum connections banate hain. Insaan akele (lonely) hote hain, aur wo chahte hain ki unhe dekha aur jaana jaye. Log kisi badi cheez ka hissa banna chahte hain. Is tarah se wo safe feel karte hain, aur aksar isme zyada maza bhi aata hai.

Hum experiences create karte hain. Kisi product ko use karna, kisi service ke sath judna. Donation dena, kisi rally mein jana, ya customer service ko call karna. Inmein se har ek action us kahani (story) ka hissa hai; har ek action hamara thoda-thoda connection banata hai. Marketers hone ke naate, hum in experiences ko ek maqsad (intent) ke sath offer kar sakte hain, jaan-bujh kar (on purpose) aisa kar sakte hain.

Poori organization marketer ke liye aur uske sath milkar kaam karti hai, kyunki marketing hi toh sab kuch hai. Hum kya banate hain, use kaise banate hain, aur kiske liye banate hain. Ye uske effects aur side effects dono hain, uski pricing (keemat) aur uska profit—sab kuch ek sath.

Market-driven: Bus kaun chala raha hai?

Har organization—har project—kisi ek main taqat (primary driving force) se chal raha hota hai.

Kuch restaurants chef-driven hote hain (yani wahan chef ki chalti hai). Silicon Valley aksar tech-driven hai. New York ki investment firms money-driven hoti hain, unka poora focus share price ya latest financial manipulations par hota hai.

Driver chahe aap jise bhi chunein, wahi wo aawaz hoti hai jo sabse saaf sunai deti hai, aur jis insaan ki wo aawaz hoti hai, wahi table ke head (sabse main kursi) par baithta hai.

Aksar, organizations marketing-driven hoti hain. Wo bahut slick (chalaak) hoti hain, unka focus bas offer par, upari chamak (surface shine) par, aur ek aur dollar (paisa) nichodne (squeeze out) ki kabiliyat par hota hai.

Main sach mein aapko marketing-driven banne mein madad karne mein interested nahi hoon, kyunki ye ek dead end (band raasta) hai.

Iska dusra raasta hai market-driven banna—market ko sunna, uski aawaz pe dhyan dena, aur isse bhi important baat, use influence karna, use thoda modna (bend it), aur use behtar banana.

Jab aap marketing-driven hote hain, toh aapka poora focus latest Facebook data hacks par, apne naye logo ki design par, aur apne naye pricing model par hota hai. Doosri taraf, jab aap market-driven hote hain, toh aap apne customers aur unke doston ki umeedon (hopes) aur sapnon (dreams) ke baare mein bahut sochte hain. Aap unki pareshaniyon (frustrations) ko sunte hain aur culture ko badalne mein invest karte hain.

Market-driven banna lamba chalta hai (lasts).

Rational Choice Ka Bhram (The myth of rational choice)

Microeconomics ek aisi baat par based hai jise aasaani se galat sabit kiya ja sakta hai: Wikipedia kehta hai, "Ek rational (samajhdar) agent se ye umeed ki jaati hai ki wo available information, events ki probabilities, aur aage hone wale nuksan/fayde (costs and benefits) ko dhyan mein rakh kar apni pasand tay karega, aur lagatar apne hisaab se sabse best action choose karega."

Bilkul nahi (Of course not).

Shayad agar hum logo ke ek bahut bade group ka average nikalein, toh ho sakta hai ki average ke hisaab se humein is tarah ke behavior ki thodi bohot jhalak dikh jaye. Par ye aisi cheez nahi hai jis par main chahunga ki aap shart lagayein (bet on).

Asal mein, aapke liye ye shart lagana zyada behtar rahega ki: "Jab bhi doubt ho, toh ye maan lijiye ki log apne current irrational urges (bina soche samjhe aane wali desires) ke hisaab se act karenge. Wo us information ko ignore kar denge jo unke beliefs ke khilaf jati hai, wo short-term faydo ke chakkar mein long-term faydo ko kurbaan kar denge, aur sabse badi baat, wo us culture se influence honge jisse wo khud ko juda hua mante hain."

Aap yahan do galtiyan (mistakes) kar sakte hain:

  1. Ye maan lena ki jin logo ko tum serve karna chahte ho wo well-informed (jaankaar) hain, rational (hamesha logic se sochne wale) hain, independent (azad) hain, aur long-term (lamba soch kar) decisions lene wale hain.
  2. Ye maan lena ki har koi tumhare jaisa hai, wahi janta hai jo tum jante ho, aur wahi chahta hai jo tum chahte ho.

"Main rational (hamesha logic se chalne wala) nahi hoon aur na hi tum ho."
